The Score — even hours, or zoomed on now?

Same horizontal-time premise.
Same five categories.
What changes is how the axis breathes. ← that's the question.
Option A Panoramic — even axis, three big staves.

The day reads as three wide, breathing staves: my-day (calendar + tasks + reading as one stave with glyphs), the system, and weather as a shaded band behind everything. Uniform hour axis. An elegant, even thing.

Strength: the eye drifts along the day; the day feels long, considered. Weakness: every hour pays the same rent; you spend pixels on 11am and 14:00 even when they're quiet.

Option B Fish-eye — uneven hours, zoomed on now.

Same staves, but the axis is non-uniform. Past hours compress to a strip on the left, the next 3 hours expand taking ~50% of the width, mid-day occupies the middle, far-future compresses on the right. The lens walks down the day with you.

Strength: the score is biased toward "what's next"; the present gets real estate proportional to its decision weight. Weakness: the axis is no longer a uniform clock — geometric comparisons across the whole day get lossy.
