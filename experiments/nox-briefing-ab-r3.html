What changes is row height. ← that's the question.
Option A Flat step — every hour the same height.

Predictable grid: the hour-axis is uniform top-to-bottom. 06 is the same size as 09 is the same size as 17. The empty hours are doing work too — they show the shape of the day, the quiet bits between the busy bits.

Strength: scannable as a list; "where in the day are we?" is answered by vertical position alone. Weakness: a lot of space spent on hours you're not in yet.

Option B Focal step — past compresses, near-future expands.

The vertical scale is a fish-eye in time. Past hours collapse to thin strips (you know they happened, you can scan them). The current hour and the next two or three are full-height. Far-future compresses again to a horizon strip. The briefing zooms on what's coming.

Strength: the rendering matches the question being asked at 07:42 — "what's next?" Weakness: the grid is no longer a clock; lookups by exact time take a beat longer.
