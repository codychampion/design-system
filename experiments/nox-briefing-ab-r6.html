Five staves equal — or one big, three small?

Same fish-eye axis.
Same day-zones.
What changes is the weight of each stave.
Option A Democratic — every stave equal.

All five staves the same height. Calendar, tasks, systems, reading, weather get the same vertical real estate. The score is a chart, not an opinion — you decide what matters.

Strength: consistent, neutral, scannable as data. Weakness: weather and reading get as much weight as the meetings; nothing pulls the eye to the things that actually decide your morning.

Option B Tiered — calendar large, others small.

Three tiers of weight. Calendar is primary — tall stave, big bars. Tasks & systems are secondary — half-height. Reading & weather are tertiary — thin marginalia strips at the foot. The score has an opinion about what matters at 07:42.

Strength: the eye lands on the meetings first; the score does triage for you. Weakness: the system enforces its idea of priority; on a quiet day you'd want weather equal to meetings.
