The band — flat across the hour, or shaped by the hour?

Same line + band.
What changes is whether the band itself has shape.
A: flat (7-day p10–p90). B: time-of-day (circadian).
Option A Flat band — one normal range across the hour.

A single rectangular shaded band — the p10–p90 of the past 7 days. The band has no shape; it's a static reference. Any deviation from "normal" reads as displacement from a flat zone.

Strength: simple, calm, easy to interpret. Weakness: ignores time-of-day: traffic at 03:00 and 14:00 don't have the same "normal."

Option B Time-of-day band — circadian, with shape.

The band itself has shape: it's the p10–p90 envelope at this time of day, computed over the past 4 weeks. Morning load curves rise into the band; quiet hours pull it down. The line is judged against the curve, not a rectangle.

Strength: a 200ms latency at 03:00 reads as anomalous; at 09:00 reads as normal — the chart knows. Weakness: more visual complexity; you're now reading two shapes, not one.
