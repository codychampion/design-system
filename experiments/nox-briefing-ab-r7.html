Filled bars, or outline-with-stems?

Same fish-eye axis, day-zones, tiered staves.
What changes is how the calendar bars are drawn.
Option A Filled bars — text inside the rectangle.

Events are solid filled rectangles; the title and time sit inside, on a tinted background. The bar's width is the duration and the bar itself carries the label. A confident, Gantt-style mark — readable, contained, recognisable.

Strength: compact; you know where the event starts and ends with no eye-travel. Weakness: short events (30m standup) crop the label; the bar shape rules the typography.

Option B Stems — label above, hairline bar below.

Events are thin outlined bars; the label rises above the bar with a small tick at the start. Like a Tufte stem-and-leaf, or annotations on a financial chart. The typography is unconstrained by the bar's width.

Strength: labels are typographically full-strength; the score reads more like a notebook annotation. Weakness: two events in the same x-zone need vertical staggering; the score wants more height per stave.
