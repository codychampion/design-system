Option A Clean foot — sign-off, links, end of page.

The page ends with a quiet sign-off line: wordmark, location, year, symbol, contact and CV links. Signal mode all the way down. Nothing borrows from obsidian.

Strength: tonal consistency; the page is exactly one register from masthead to foot. Weakness: a recruiter checking "is this person currently shipping?" has nothing to anchor to.

Option B Obsidian postscript — a live "now" block at the foot.

The page ends with a small obsidian-mode "what's live right now" block — three short fields in mono: shipping this week, reading, availability. Reads like a status line at the bottom of a Stripe Press book — the author's working terminal poking through the page.

Strength: the page proves the author is currently active; the cross-mode contrast is itself a signal. Bridges signal and obsidian; doesn't break the rule that says a page is in one mode. Weakness: mode discipline gets fuzzier; not every viewer will read it as deliberate.
