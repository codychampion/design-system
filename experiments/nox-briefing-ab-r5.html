The Fish-eye — does the lens follow you, or does the day have fixed zones?

Same five staves.
Same non-uniform axis.
What changes is what the lens is shaped by.
Option A Lens follows now — reactive, walks with you.

The zoomed band is always centred on the current time, ±2 hours. As the morning passes, the lens slides right with you. At 14:00 the lens would be over 12–16; at 17:00 over 15–19. The score is always about "the next thing."

Strength: the rendering is reactive to NOW; you never look at a score that's biased to a moment that's already past. Weakness: harder to develop muscle memory — the same hour sits in a different x-position depending when you look.

Option B Day-zones — the day has named regions.

The axis has named zones with fixed widths: overnight · morning · midday · afternoon · after-hours. Zone sizes reflect typical importance, not where you are. 09:00 always sits in the same x-position on the page; the score develops muscle memory.

Strength: stable mental model. The 1:1 with the EM lives in morning, the design review in afternoon, every day. Weakness: the visual emphasis is set by the system's idea of a typical day, not the actual moment.
