Where do the pulse-lines live?

Same log. Same data.
Question: where do the time-series pulses sit relative to the stream?
A: above. B: beside.
Option A Pulses above — a vital-signs strip, then the log.

A small-multiples strip of pulse-lines sits above the log: one mini-sparkline per service, last hour, with current value. The log streams below. You glance up for "how are vitals?", glance down for "what just happened?"

Strength: two distinct reading zones; pulses are scannable as a row. Weakness: pulses are above the fold, the log is below; you can't see both at once for a small viewport.

Option B Pulses beside — companion column to the log.

The log dominates the main column. A narrow right margin shows live pulses for the most relevant services (auto-sorted by severity). Pulses are companions to the log, not a summary; they share the page, you read them in parallel.

Strength: log and pulses are visible together; the eye triangulates. Weakness: margin only fits 4-5 pulses; less-relevant services need to be browsed elsewhere.
