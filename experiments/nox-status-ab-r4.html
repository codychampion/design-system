The pulses — drawn as lines, or as bars?

Same tiered strip, same hero/sub/mini.
What changes is how each pulse is rendered.
A: continuous polyline. B: discrete status-stripe bars.
Option A Lines — continuous polylines, EKG-style.

Each pulse is a continuous polyline over the time window — values rise and fall smoothly. The shape of the line carries the story; the flatline means flatline.

Strength: visceral medical reference (heartbeat / EKG). Trend and slope are unmistakable. Weakness: "is it currently up?" requires looking at the right edge; binary up/down isn't the language.

Option B Bars — discrete status columns, statuspage-style.

Each pulse is a row of discrete columns, one per time-bucket (5 min for hero, 10 min for sub, 30 min for mini). Each column is coloured by state — ok, warn, err, dim if no data. State per interval, not trend.

Strength: "was it up at 09:35?" is one glance. The shape of an incident reads as a wall of red. Weakness: slope and magnitude are lost; "going up or down?" is harder to tell.
