The Daybook — does the day read as one stream, or two?

Same time-spine in both.
Same content (events · tasks · alerts · pings · weather).
What hangs from the rail differs. ← that's the question.
Option A The Log — one stream, everything interleaved.

Every item — your meetings, your tasks, NOX's pings, the weather forecast, reading queue — flows down one column in chronological order. The day is the layout, and the day is one thing. Each row uses a small leading glyph to signal type (calendar event, system ping, task, etc) but otherwise reads as a single log.

Strength: simple, scannable, one place to look. Weakness: "what was NOX doing while I was in standup?" requires reading carefully.

Option B Two days, one hour — parallel rails for me & the system.

Same hour-axis runs down the spine. Now there are two synchronised columns: my day (calendar, tasks, reading) on the left, the system day (services, alerts, jobs, weather) on the right. The eye reads horizontally to see "what was happening while I was happening."

Strength: sync is the design — meeting at 09:00 sits next to "etl.briefing slow 09:42" at a glance. Weakness: denser, asymmetric (one column often heavier than the other).
