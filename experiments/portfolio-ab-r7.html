R7 · row ↔ case study — pick below
Option A Silent door — the row promises nothing; the room is the case study.

Each row is exactly what it looks like: title, date, tag, arrow. No hover preview, no peek, no expand. The case study lives one click away — the manifest doesn't try to sell the trip. Trust the title to do its job.

Strength: the manifest stays a tight index. Nothing competes with the present-year rows for attention. The page is exactly one read-length. Weakness: the reader has to commit one click before they learn anything beyond the metrics. No editorial voice between row and room.

Option B Editorial peek — hover the row, a sentence about the room appears.

Each row gains one italic Lora line, set in the title column, hidden until hover. It's editorial voice — the kind of sentence a magazine editor might pull from the body to invite the reader in. The manifest doesn't reflow; the peek slides into existing slack.

Strength: the manifest gains a register without gaining chrome. Voice travels from the preamble into the index itself; the cross-row rhythm earns a heartbeat. Weakness: the peek is voice-loaded — every row needs a sentence worth reading. Bad peek copy makes the whole manifest feel tinny.
