How hard does the past recede on the manifest?

Open question from the prefs log:
"Past states should recede visibly, not just lighten." Applied to the manifest, that's a choice with teeth. Today's work loudest; yesteryear's work quiet.

R5 · temporal weight — pick below
Option A Even hand — year-grouped, weight uniform.

The manifest is one register. Every year reads at the same volume. Older entries lose their summary line, but type size, weight, and colour stay put. The year strip is the only structural cue that time is passing.

Strength: the work is presented democratically — 2022 has as much right to be seen as 2025. Weakness: contradicts the prefs-log principle. A recruiter scanning for "currently shipping" gets no visual answer; they have to read the dates.

Option B Past wash — time has gravity; the present sits loudest.

Each year recedes one stop in size, weight, and saturation. 2025 is the loudest type on the page. 2024 reads at body weight. 2023+ shrinks to a quiet caption row. The accent (live, ◆ blue) belongs only to the current year. Hover restores the past to full legibility — the work isn't hidden, just demoted.

Strength: the page itself answers "is this person currently shipping?" before the reader has read a word. Past is honoured but doesn't shout over now. Weakness: someone scanning for a specific older project (say, the 2022 causal work) has more to do; the hover-to-restore is a small ask but it is an ask.
