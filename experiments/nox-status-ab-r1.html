What kind of object is the status page?

Same data, two premises.
A: stream · what just happened, chronologically.
B: chart · how each service is doing, by row.
Both perpendicular to the briefing's Score.
Option A The Audit Log — a tail-f of the day.

A reverse-chronological stream. Most recent at top, live ones pulse. The page reads as a single dense column of evidence — what just happened, in order. The status is the trail, not the snapshot.

Strength: answers "what just happened?" instantly. Single scroll; no widgets. Weakness: "is X service healthy?" requires reading; you can't see all services at a glance.

Option B The Hospital Chart — services as patients with vitals.

Each service is a row with vitals: name, current status, last-hour pulse-line, P99, error rate, attending notes. Sorted by severity, critical first. The chart is a doctor's clipboard, not a stream.

Strength: "is X working?" at a glance. Each service has one row; pulses show whether things are stable or deteriorating. Weakness: "what just happened?" requires drilling into a row.
