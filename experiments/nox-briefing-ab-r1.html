Option A The Daybook — a vertical timeline as the spine.

Every item — events, services pinging, weather, task deadlines — hangs off a single ruled hour-scale running down the page. The day is the layout. The now line is the only striking element: a thin gold horizontal mark across the timeline at the current time. Reads like a field-log of a morning, not a dashboard.

Refuses: sidebar nav · stat ribbon at top · service panel as separate widget · cards · "now/upcoming" sections. Reach: Tufte small-multiples in time; an FT live-day timeline; a lab notebook with hourly entries.

Option B The Broadsheet — a morning paper, set for one reader.

A four-column-feel morning edition with masthead, dateline, a single pulled lede for the most urgent item, and three columns of dispatches under category headlines. The alert is news, not a banner. Reads like the front page of a small paper printed for an audience of one.

Refuses: sidebars · stat tiles · service-list panels · timeline. Reach: the FT front page · a Wall Street Journal "What's News" rail · the printed Economist; not a SaaS dashboard.
