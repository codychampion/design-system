Where does the voice live in the Manifest?

Same time-led work index.
What changes is how voice creeps in.
A: voice at the top only. B: voice in every entry too.
Option A Voice at the top — short preamble, then a clean manifest.

A three-paragraph preamble at the top of the page — voice, claim, evidence in 90 seconds. Below it: a clean dated manifest. Each entry stays terse: time, title, one-line summary. The preamble is the profile; the manifest is the work; they don't overlap.

Strength: two distinct reading registers — read the preamble for "who", scan the manifest for "what". Recruiter scans the bottom; a peer reads from the top. Weakness: manifest entries feel inventory-like; the voice doesn't reach the work.

Option B Voice throughout — preamble + a sentence per ship.

Shorter preamble — two paragraphs, plus the claim. Then the manifest, but each entry carries a short editorial sentence under the summary: an honest line about what it was actually like, or what didn't work, or what it taught you. The voice reaches the work.

Strength: the manifest sounds like you at every entry; the work has interiority. Reads like a Stripe Press case-study reel. Weakness: each entry is heavier; the page is denser; scanning is harder.
