Prefaceiii
Preface

What the work is for, and what it refuses to do.

This book is a personal system. It exists because I read interfaces for a living and have grown tired of mine looking like everybody else's. The fault is not in the tools. The fault is in the defaults. The defaults are very good at producing something competent within a narrow band of taste, and after enough hours of competence the band itself becomes a smell.

The argument here is that a small number of structural commitments — three modes of address, a tight type scale, low border-radius, motion that is slower than reflex — do more to establish character than any amount of surface decoration. The work is not to find a better gradient. The work is to commit to a set of refusals and then to write the prose of the interface in light of them.1

There are three modes here, with the names and the references kept short so they can be invoked in conversation: obsidian, signal, field. The references are physical objects in physical rooms — a Bloomberg terminal at two in the morning, a Stripe Press book on a clean desk, a naturalist's journal on the corner of a workbench. They are not themes; that word always reduces to colour. They are postures.

Everything else in this volume follows from those three postures. The type, the colour, the spacing, the rules of imagery, the prose register — they are derivations, not separate decisions.

i · Three modes3
Chapter one

Three modes, one author. Same hand, different rooms.

A mode is the room the work is being read in. The same author makes very different sentences in a board memo, a notebook, and a status console; the same author makes very different interfaces, too, and the cost of pretending otherwise is that everything starts to look like the same vague, considered SaaS page. The three modes here are the three rooms I find myself working in. They are mutually exclusive at the surface level — a page is in one mode, not two — though some artefacts will carry a small element from a neighbouring mode (a status widget in signal, a pull quote in obsidian) when the surface calls for it.

obsidian — the operations register.

obsidian is for NOX dashboards, homelab tooling, and any Artifact I will be the only person to use. The reference is a Bloomberg terminal at two in the morning: density without apology, monospace dominant, status visible at all times, pure warm-dark behind the work. Nothing on the screen that isn't earning its place.

signal — the considered register.

signal is for anything a colleague, client, or recruiter might read. It is what this book is set in. The reference is a Stripe Press volume on a clean desk: typographically rigorous, whitespace as emphasis, photography only when it earns its place. Serious without being stiff.

field — the journal register.

field is for long-form writing, the personal site, the commonplace book. The reference is a naturalist's journal on the corner of a workbench: leather-bound, annotated in the margins, technical diagrams alongside personal observations. Tufte shelved next to Darwin's notebooks. The constraint relaxes only enough to let a person through.

ii · Typography9
Chapter two

Typography — the UI before the UI.

The system has one scale and three voices. The scale is a Major Third (1·25) over a 16-pixel base. Headings sit at weights 500 to 600; body sits at 400. The system permits two typefaces per surface, never three. Monospace is a voice, not a fallback.

The voices, by mode: obsidian in IBM Plex Mono & Plex Sans; signal in Fraunces, Lora and DM Sans; field in Newsreader, Literata and Karla. The body measure is 68 characters in signal and field; obsidian permits density and ignores this.

A small scale, with two near-display sizes. In a printed book the largest size at which a sentence really begins to feel like sound rather than language is around 49 points; everything above that is poster, not page.

Margin notes — ii Line height: 1·55 for body, 1·15 for headings, 1·00 for display. Maximum two typefaces on any surface. Monospace counts. Heading weights never exceed 600, except in genuine display contexts. Fonts loaded as <link>, never as @import (slow first-paint).
iii · Colour17
Chapter three

Colour, named not seen.

The fourteen colour roles are invariant: --c-bg, --c-text, --c-accent, and so on through their friends. The values change per mode; the names never do. This is what makes a component portable from one mode to another by swapping a single CSS variable file.

The character of each palette is doing most of the work below the level of any single component. obsidian — Monokai Dusk — sits on a warm dark #2D2A2E base with a single sharp accent in Monokai orange. signal sits on a warm off-white #f8f7f4 with a decisive blue reserved for links, CTAs and one anchor per page. field, the journal mode, lays warm gold on deep ink — gold is for pull quotes, section breaks, and one CTA; never as a wash.

iv · Measure23
Chapter four

Measure, spacing & motion.

Spacing is the silent scaffolding. Where a border is reaching to do work, spacing should be doing it instead. The scale is in fours: --s-1 at 4px through --s-24 at 96. Every padding, gap and margin in the system comes from this scale; absolute pixel values inside components are an anti-pattern with no exceptions.

Border radius — low bias.

High radius reads consumer. The ceiling for any non-pill element is --r-md at 6px; inputs and tags sit at --r-sm at 3px; modals at --r-lg at 12px. Pills and avatars only use --r-full. Cards with 16px or 24px radii are out.

Motion — slower than reflex.

Stillness is the default; motion is the signal. There is one easing curve in the system — cubic-bezier(0·16, 1, 0·3, 1), fast out, smooth deceleration — and three durations: 200 ms for micro-interactions, 350 ms for components, 600 ms for page-level transitions. The system refuses bounce, elastic, decorative looping, and "reveal on scroll" applied universally. When the system wants to draw attention somewhere, everything else stays still.

Loading states are designed, not defaulted. Skeletons matching the eventual content shape, with a slow shimmer at a 1·5-second cycle, replace any spinning circle. Progress bars where progress is actually known. prefers-reduced-motion is honoured everywhere — animations collapse to instant state changes, preserving only motion that conveys essential information.

Margin notes — iv Container max-width: 1280.
Readable measure: 68ch.
Columns: 4 / 8 / 12 at three breakpoints. Shadows: prefer spacing first, then borders, then shadow. Three levels exist (L1 / L2 / L3); most surfaces use L0. No hover animation may cause layout shift. No scale beyond 1·02. No translate beyond 2px.
v · Marks31
Chapter five

The crest, the wordmark, the symbol.

The identity system is three-tiered. The crest is universal: the Champion family mark, gold-on-black in its native rendering, recoloured by mode for operational use. It is heritage as structure. The wordmark is per-mode: the operational signature for daily use. The symbol is also per-mode: typographic punctuation for section breaks and end-of-article marks. Each tier has a different job and they do not collapse.

Plate IV · the crest, native rendering

A hexagonal frame for the molecular reference; the fox for cunning; the trident-with-arrows for the surname's literal meaning; the crystalline staff for scholarship. Rendered via PNG mask + background-color for per-mode tinting in CSS.

Chapter six

Voice — three registers, one author.

The same person writes a status line at 02:00, a memo at 14:30, and an essay on a Sunday morning, and the three are recognisable as one hand. The discipline is to know which register the surface is in, and write to it without drift. viii · Refusals55
Chapter eight

What this work is not.

This chapter is the most important one in the volume. The source document's Uniqueness Mandate exists because most AI-generated UI looks the same. The defaults converge on a narrow band of taste — cards with rounded corners, a sans-serif at the weight Claude favours, a soft gradient hero, a colour palette somewhere between tech-startup teal and corporate blue — and the only way to step out of that band is to refuse it explicitly, in writing, before generating anything.

What follows is the refusals list, set as continuous prose so it cannot be turned back into bullet-points the moment it leaves the page.

The system does not produce purple-to-pink gradient text on hero headers. It does not use glassmorphism or backdrop-blur as a primary surface treatment. It refuses Lucide icons inside coloured gradient squares with rounded-2xl corners; it refuses the bento grid as a reflex; it refuses the Inter + gray-900 + rounded-xl + shadow-2xl stack. It does not print logo strips beneath a "Trusted by" line. It refuses the twin-column hero — bold left heading paired with an abstract gradient blob on the right. It declines, as a class, three-column feature grids of identical-height cards. It refuses the floating "Get started for free" green-pill CTA, the dot-grid hero background, and any AI-generated mesh gradient as a backdrop.

Asymmetry, instead, over balance — not centring everything by default; intentional weight on one side; deliberate negative space. Editorial typography over UI typography — a magazine or a scientific journal as the reference, not Linear or Vercel. Physical-world references over screen-world references — a terminal, a notebook, a lab logbook, a periodical; not "modern SaaS." Density over emptiness. One striking element per page, not five. Real copy over placeholder voice: "Morning briefing — 07:42" beats "Welcome to your dashboard."

The smell test, when in doubt: could this be mistaken for a v0 or Lovable export? If the answer is yes, redesign — not iterate. The hard rule, when three or more refusals on the list are violated at once, is to discard and restart from a different structural premise. Iterating on the surface will not fix it. The foundation is the problem.

"Restraint as a feature. When in doubt, remove."

The current edition is set as a printed-book interior in deliberate refusal of those defaults. Earlier draft kept at index-v0.9-saas.html as an errata reference.

Colophon63
Colophon

How this volume was set.

This is the spec book for the Champion personal design system, volume 0·9 — a living document. The chapters are set in Fraunces for display and Lora for body, with margin notes and labels in DM Sans and code in DM Mono; the appendix interactively re-skins the book in obsidian (IBM Plex) or field (Newsreader · Literata · Karla) when invoked.

The crest is the Champion family mark, rendered from a single transparent PNG asset (assets/crest_white.png) recoloured at runtime via CSS mask-image + background-color; the native gold-on-black rendering (assets/crest_dark.png) appears once, on the title page of Chapter Five.

The volume is hand-typeset; there is no generator. Errors and notes go to . The next edition — 1·0 — folds in print rules, email signatures, slide-deck templates and a more complete component reference. None of those exist yet.
